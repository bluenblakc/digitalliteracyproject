Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. This is usually done by sending fake emails, messages, or links that appear to come from trusted sources like banks or popular websites.

A common phishing scenario begins with a user receiving an email claiming that their bank account needs verification. The email contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks similar to the official one. When the user enters their login credentials, the attacker captures this information. The attacker can then access the user’s account and perform unauthorized transactions.

Students and young internet users are often targeted because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links and verifying sources before sharing any personal information online.
